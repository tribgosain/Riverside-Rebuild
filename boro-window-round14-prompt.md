# Riverside Rebuild — Round 14: Heraldic Lion Mark (Replaces the Bridge)

Paste into Claude Code. Replaces the header badge and favicon entirely —
the transporter bridge attempt is retired, not worth further rounds on a
structure that's proven not to compress well to icon size.

## What to build

An **original heraldic lion silhouette** — rampant pose (standing on hind
legs, one foreleg raised, side-on profile), mane, curved back, tail. Solid
**filled shape in red** (not black — this should be the same red used for
"REBUILD" in the wordmark), not multi-stroke line art.

**Important — this must be visually distinct from Middlesbrough FC's
actual crest lion**, not a redrawn copy of it. This is an unofficial fan
project (the app already disclaims that in the footer) — the lion here is
a generic heraldic device referencing the broader tradition of lions in
football crests and heraldry generally, not a recreation of the specific
club's registered mark. Keep the pose, linework, and overall style
clearly your own — don't reference or approximate the actual MFC crest
artwork while building this.

**Why filled silhouette, not line art:** the bridge attempts failed
repeatedly because multi-part line constructions (separate strokes that
need to align precisely — towers, a truss, a gondola, all as distinct
paths) are fragile at small size and easy to get subtly wrong. A single
solid silhouette shape doesn't have that failure mode — there's one shape,
either it looks like a lion or it doesn't, no separate pieces to lose or
misalign. This should be a more reliable build, not just a different one.

## Sizing

- **Header badge**: the filled lion silhouette, red, sized to sit
  comfortably next to "RIVERSIDE REBUILD" the way the bridge mark did
  before.
- **Favicon**: same silhouette, same red, tested at actual 16px — a solid
  filled shape should hold up better here than the bridge's linework did,
  but still verify it actually reads as a lion at that size and isn't just
  a red blob. If it's not legible at 16px, simplify the silhouette
  further (fewer mane details, cleaner leg/tail shapes) rather than
  shrinking a detailed version down.
